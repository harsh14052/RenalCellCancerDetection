Renal Cell Carcinoma Diagnosis Using Machine Learning

Renal Cell Carcinoma

Renal Cell Cancer, also known as kidney cancer, is a disease in which maligant(cancer) cells are detected in the lining of the kidney tubules.

  • According to GLOBOCAN(Global Cancer Oberservatory) 2020, kidney cancer is the 14th and 15th most common cause of new cases and fatalities, respectively.
  • Every year, kidney cancer accounts for 1,44,000 fatalities and 3,38,000 new cases worldwide.
  • In India, the projected incidence of RCC among males is about 2/1,00,000 population while among female is about 1/1,00,000.

Types of Renal Cell Carcinoma

  1. Clear Cell : approximately 75% of individual cells appear very pale or clear under the microscope.
  2. Papillary : approximately 10% to 15% of people have this form. These cancers form little finger like projections(called papillae).
  3. Chromophobe : approximately 5% of cases. These cells are larger and have prominent cell borders and can appear pink or clear.
  4. Unclassified : approximately 5% of tumors that do not fit into any other category.
